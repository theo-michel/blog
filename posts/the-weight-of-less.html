The Weight of Less

December 2025

Every object you own has weight. Not just physical weight—mental weight. It occupies space in your mind, demands maintenance, creates decisions.

I used to think more options meant more freedom. But options are obligations in disguise. Each one asks: choose me, organize me, remember me.

There's a lightness that comes from having exactly what you need. Not less than you need—that's deprivation. Exactly what you need. That's freedom.

The things you keep should earn their place.
