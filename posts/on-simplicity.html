On Simplicity

January 2026

The best part is no part. The best process is no process. It weighs nothing, costs nothing, can't go wrong.

We spend so much time adding. Features, options, configurations. Each addition feels like progress. But complexity compounds. Every new element interacts with every other element.

Simplicity isn't the absence of sophistication. It's the result of it. You have to understand something deeply before you can express it simply.

The goal isn't minimalism for its own sake. It's clarity. When you remove the unnecessary, what remains speaks louder.
